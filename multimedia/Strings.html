The string section of an orchestra is the biggest of the sections. It consists of mainly violins (left picture), violas, cellos (middle picture), and double basses (right picture). The way all of these instruments look is mostly the same, with their size being the main differential factor. The bigger the instrument, the lower the range. All of these instruments are played using a bow, and the strings on the neck of the instruments are pressed to change the pitch. Violins and violas are smaller so are held resting on your shoulder, whereas cellos and double basses are rested on the floor.
