The percussion section consists of the most amount of different instruments in an orchestra. These instruments are extremely varied, and include anything that can be hit or shaken to produce a sound. Some percussion instruments include timpani drums (left picture), xylophone (middle picture), cymbals (right picture), snare drum, tambourine, triangle, and chimes. The percussion section maintains rhythm, while adding exciting, unusual sounds to music. While some percussion instruments are tuned (e.g. xylophone, timpani), others have no set pitch (e.g. cymbals, triangle, tambourine).
